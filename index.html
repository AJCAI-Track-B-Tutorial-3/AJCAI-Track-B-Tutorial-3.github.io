Reinforcement Learning:
Single and Multi-Agent Foundations and Applications

Track B Tutorial 3
2 December, 13:45–16:00 · Cinema, The Australian National University (ANU), Canberra

About the Tutorial

Reinforcement Learning (RL) provides a principled framework for sequential decision-making and is foundational to many modern AI systems. This tutorial introduces the core concepts of RL—including Markov decision processes, value functions, and deep RL methods such as DQN, policy gradients, and actor–critic algorithms—highlighting how these approaches enable agents to learn effectively in complex environments.

Building on these foundations, we extend to Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL), where multiple agents interact, cooperate, or compete. We cover key paradigms such as Centralised Training with Decentralised Execution (CTDE), major benchmarks, and emerging research directions involving scalability, coordination, heterogeneity, and links to large language models. The tutorial equips participants with a structured understanding of RL and MARL, preparing them to engage with current research challenges and real-world applications.
